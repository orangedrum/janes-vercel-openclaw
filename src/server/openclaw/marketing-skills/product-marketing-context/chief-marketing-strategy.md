# Chief Marketing Strategy Guide

This document unifies the core principles behind the marketing skills in this repo.
It blends the practical positioning and dream audience focus of Daniel Priestley with the offer, copy, and testing discipline of Aaron Fletcher.

## Core principles

### 1. Perfect Targeting first
- Know your ideal customer in detail.
- Be very specific: role, company type, problem, buying trigger.
- Use the "Dream 100" style mindset: focus on the best 10-20 accounts before scaling.
- Every lead batch must be built around a clearly defined ICP.

### 2. Perfect Offer second
- Your offer should be simple, specific, and easy to say.
- It should solve a clear, urgent problem and be framed as a small next step.
- The offer is not features; it is the outcome and reduction of risk.
- Every outreach sequence needs a single, clear micro-ask.

### 3. Perfect Message third
- Write as if you are speaking to one person.
- Use customer language and real signals.
- Keep it short, conversational, and trustworthy.
- Avoid generic hype; focus on relevance and credibility.

### 4. Authority and trust
- Build authority through proof points, not jargon.
- Use social proof, specific results, and identifiable context.
- Show confidence without overselling.

### 5. Iterate quickly
- Test one variable at a time.
- Learn from real responses and adjust the next batch.
- A good strategy is better than more volume.

### 6. Use structure over guesswork
- Capture context, positioning, objections, and switching dynamics.
- Use clear frameworks for offers, email copy, and follow-up.
- Document what works and why.

## How this applies to the repo

### Product Marketing Context
- The `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` file is the single source of truth.
- It must include ICP, offer, proof points, objections, and customer language.
- This follows Priestley-style positioning and Fletcher-style conversion clarity.

### Lead and list creation
- Free-lead-discovery and lead-batch-create should focus on highly relevant prospect signals.
- Seek quality over quantity: better leads with stronger signals beat a larger weak list.
- The list should support the offer and message, not the other way around.

### Email validation
- Validate lists with trusted hygiene checks before sending.
- Do not rely on unverified third-party promises.
- A clean list is a necessary foundation for both Fletcher-style small offers and Priestley-style authority.

### Send strategy and testing
- Use small batches and clear hypotheses.
- Keep email sequences short and ask for one thing.
- Evaluate results, then refine subject, hook, or CTA.

## What to avoid
- Building large cold lists without a tracked offer.
- Sending long, generic copy to unverified contacts.
- Treating warmup or validation as a substitute for relevance.
- Changing too many variables at once.

## Practical tactical rules for the skills
- `product-marketing-context`: capture the perfect customer, the exact offer, and the one reason they should act.
- `lead-batch-create`: build the list around the offer and the most relevant signals.
- `email-validation`: keep the list clean so every send is more effective.
- `lead-batch-testing`: test one thing, measure one thing, and improve the next batch.

## Summary
This repo should behave like a single strategist.
Every skill is a different tool, but they all work toward the same goal:
- precise targeting,
- a tight offer,
- personalized message,
- and steady learning from real results.
